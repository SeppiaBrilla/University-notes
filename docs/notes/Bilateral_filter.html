Bilateral filter

It is a non-linear Image filters that deals with Gaussian noise without blurring the image like Mean filter and Gaussian filter . The formula of a bilateral filter is: O(p)=∑q∈SH(p,q)∗IqH(p,q)=1W(p)Gσs(ds(p,q))Gσr(dr(p,q))ds(p,q)=∣∣p−q∣∣2=(up−uq)2−(vp−vq)2→spatial distancedr(Ip,Iq)=∣Ip−Iq∣→range/intensity distanceW(p)=∑q∈sGσs(ds(p,q))Gσr(dr(p,q))→normalization factor \begin{align} &O(p) = \sum_{q \in S} H(p,q) *I_q\\ &H(p,q) = \frac{1}{W(p)}G_{\sigma_s}(d_s(p,q))G_{\sigma_r}(d_r(p,q))\\\\ &d_s(p,q) = ||p - q||_2 = \sqrt{(u_p - u_q)^2 - (v_p - v_q)^2} \rightarrow \text{spatial distance}\\ &d_r(I_p,I_q) = |I_p - I_q| \rightarrow \text{range/intensity distance}\\ &W(p) = \sum_{q \in s} G_{\sigma_s}(d_s(p,q))G_{\sigma_r}(d_r(p,q)) \rightarrow \text{normalization factor} \end{align} ​O(p)=q∈S∑​H(p,q)∗Iq​H(p,q)=W(p)1​Gσs​​(ds​(p,q))Gσr​​(dr​(p,q))ds​(p,q)=∣∣p−q∣∣2​=(up​−uq​)2−(vp​−vq​)2​→spatial distancedr​(Ip​,Iq​)=∣Ip​−Iq​∣→range/intensity distanceW(p)=q∈s∑​Gσs​​(ds​(p,q))Gσr​​(dr​(p,q))→normalization factor​​

In practice, the filter takes into account both the spatial and the color distance of the pixels and uses it to increase or lower the influence of the neighbors pixels on the intensity of the current one.
