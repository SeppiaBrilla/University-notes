Any linear system can be solved by calculating the inverse of the matrix AAA: Ax=b⇒A−1Ax=A−1b⇒x=A−1b Ax=b \Rightarrow A^{-1}Ax = A^{-1}b \Rightarrow x = A^{-1}b Ax=b⇒A−1Ax=A−1b⇒x=A−1b To solve a linear square system using a direct method we first compute the "LU" maxtrix factorization then solve two triangular system: Ax=bA=LULy=bUx=y \begin{align} &Ax = b\\ &A = LU\\ &Ly = b\\ &Ux = y \end{align} ​Ax=bA=LULy=bUx=y​​ Where LLL is a lower triangular matrix [x1100…0x21x220…0……………xn1xn2xn3…xnn] \begin{bmatrix} x_{11} & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ x_{21} & x_{22} & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ \dots &\dots & \dots & \dots & \dots \\ x_{n1} & x_{n2} & x_{n3} & \dots & x_{nn} \end{bmatrix} ​x11​x21​…xn1​​0x22​…xn2​​00…xn3​​…………​00…xnn​​​ And UUU is an upper triangular matrix [x11x12x13…x1n0x22x23…x2n……………000…xnn] \begin{bmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12} & x_{13} & \dots & x_{1n} \\ 0 & x_{22} & x_{23} & \dots & x_{2n} \\ \dots &\dots & \dots & \dots & \dots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & x_{nn} \end{bmatrix} ​x11​0…0​x12​x22​…0​x13​x23​…0​…………​x1n​x2n​…xnn​​​ Solving a triangular system costs O(n22)O(\frac{n^2}{2})O(2n2​) and the computation of the inverse matrix A−1A^{-1}A−1 costs O(n3)O(n^3)O(n3)

The LU factorization can't be performed if the matrix AAA is non-singular and the algorithm is unstable . In order to solve those problems we can perform the pivoting factorization PA=LUPA = LUPA=LU where PPP is a permutation matrix . The pivoting factorization is stable and can be performed on any non-singular matrix. The cost of resolving a linear system with the pivoting factorization is O(23n3)O(\frac{2}{3}n^3)O(32​n3)
