EUF theory

EUF = Equality with Uninterpreted Functions theory, is a way to reason over problems without assuming what the functions do. It is useful when we are dealing with functions very difficult to compute. Example: a∗(f(b)+f(c))=d∧b∗(f(a)+f(c))≠d∧a=b a * (f(b) + f(c)) = d \wedge b * (f(a) + f(c)) \neq d \wedge a = b a∗(f(b)+f(c))=d∧b∗(f(a)+f(c))=d∧a=b In this case we do not need arithmetic theory to understand that this is unsatisfiable. It is a problem we can solve with Satisfiability Modulo Theory (SMT) .
