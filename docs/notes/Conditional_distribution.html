Conditional distribution

Given two events A,BA, BA,B we denote as P(B)P(B)P(B) the Probability of BBB. But, if we assume correlation between the two events we may want to know what happend to the Probability of BBB if the event AAA occour (or is assumed it has). We can call that the conditional probability P(B∣A)P(B|A)P(B∣A), the Probability of BBB given AAA. and it is computed as: P(B∣A)=P(A∩B)P(A) P(B|A) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)} P(B∣A)=P(A)P(A∩B)​
